    - he is wrongfully kicked out (israelites worship other gods)

jephthah's relationship with his own people and family mirrors the one god has with israelites, his children. he is wrongfully kicked out of his own home because he was a son of another mother an aspect he cannot control. this is similar to how israelites, god's own children, forsake god by worshipping other gods and sinning. elders of gilead begs jephthah to come back and fight the ammonites mirroring how israelites plead to god once oppression gets bad for them. jephthah even though he was treated unfairly he still helps just as god did to israel by raising judges. to put the cherry on top, jephthah must sacrifice his own daughter just as god did to his only son jesus.

바울은 공회에게 양심을 따라 하느님을 섬겼노라라고 당당히 외친후 데제사장이 바울의 입을 때립니다. 그후 바울은 아나니아에게 reacts strongly. instead of keeping his calm, he almost curses at the council showing that paul is still human. what is more important though is that he apologizes immediately after learning that he has reacted strongly at the high priest. despite being hit, he still shows humility and respect towards the high priest by not justifying his anger but aligning himself with god 

paul witnesses jesus who tells him that his mission is unfinished and that he must continue spreading gospel in rome instead similar to how jesus spoke to him in yesterday's passage. it is as if jesus himself recognized that these chaotic situations was too much for even paul himself that he had to tell this to himself so he will make the right decisions 

many times in my life have i said harsh things to others and never apologized to them. it takes a lot of humility and self-control to apologize after being struct in the mouth for defending oneself. what is clear from paul's apology is that it shows that paul is willing to bring himself down to move the situation forward. by apologizing immediately, he opens an opportunity to speak more instead of being beaten or disagreed upon or worse starting a fight. he is so determined with his mission that he immediately recognizes his fault and apologizes to not further waste paul and the high priest's time. i pray that i may recognize my faults at times where i have reacted unfairly to others and react as paul did to move the situation forward instead of causing another chaotic scene.

i have yet to witness god's prescence or let alone hear his words. this was the reason why i abandoned studying the bible at one point -- i gave up on my faith as i thought he would never listen. it still seems like for me that if i were to thank god for an event, it seems like i'm thanking him for something that happened randomly. i used to believe this until this year. attending church, meeting good people, reading about paul's journey so far has made me positive that i would hear his words and that i would recognize what to do when it happens. even though i am not yet a full believer, i believe that learning more about jesus has made me think deeply about the fact that i should react as paul did -- recognizing when you have crossed a line and being humble when you can. 





바울은 공회 앞에서 자신이 양심을 따라 하나님을 섬겨왔다고 당당히 말했습니다. 그러자 대제사장이 바울의 입을 치라고 명령했습니다. 바울은 그 말을 듣고 화를 내며 아나니아를 강하게 꾸짖습니다. 침착함을 잃은 모습이지만 바울도 여전히 인간적인 감정을 표현할때도있다라는 것을 보여줍니다. 하지만 중요한 점은 바울이 곧바로 자신의 행동을 인정하고 사과했다는 것입니다. 자신을 때린 상황에서도 분노를 정당화하지 않고, 하나님의 말씀에 따라 겸손히 태도를 바꿨습니다.

그날 밤, 예수님이 바울에게 나타나 “네가 예루살렘에서 나를 증언한 것처럼 로마에서도 증언해야 한다”라고 말씀하십니다. 어제의 말씀처럼, 예수님은 바울에게 그의 사명이 아직 끝나지 않았다고 알려주십니다. 혼란스럽고 어려운 상황 속에서도 예수님은 바울에게 직접 말씀하시며 힘을 주셨습니다. 바울이 드디어 침착함을 잃었을때 직접 오신게 우연의 일치가 아닌 것 같았습니다.

나도 내 삶에서 누군가에게 상처 주는 말을 하고도 사과하지 않은 적이 많습니다. 하지만 바울처럼 자신이 잘못했다는 것을 인정하고, 그 자리에서 사과하는 것은 정말 큰 겸손과 자기 절제가 필요한거같은데 저는 그 면이 매우 부족합니다. 바울의 행동에서 볼 수 있듯이, 사과는 단순한 말이 아니라 어려운 상황을 앞으로 나아가게 하는 힘이 있습니다. 바울은 자신의 사명을 위해 싸움을 피하고, 대화의 길을 열기 위해 곧바로 사과했습니다.

나도 누군가에게 부당하게 반응했을 때, 내 잘못을 빨리 깨닫고 바울처럼 겸손히 사과할 수 있는 사람이 되길 기도합니다. 그렇게 함으로써 더 큰 다툼이 생기지 않고, 오히려 하나님께서 원하시는 평화로운 방향으로 상황이 흘러가길 바랍니다.
